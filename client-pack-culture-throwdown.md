### WHAT TO EXPECT ON THE DAY

You and your team will arrive to a game-show atmosphere — tables set with scorecards, a live leaderboard ready to roll, and a high-energy MC warming up the room. Culture Throwdown is exactly what it sounds like: a fast-paced, competitive game-show experience where teams face off through culture trivia, values dilemmas, read-the-room scenarios, and live debates. It is electric from the moment it starts.

You'll be grouped into teams of five to eight people and seated at your tables. Then the rounds begin. Culture trivia challenges what your team actually knows about workplace behaviour and company values. Values dilemma rounds force genuine discussion — there is no obvious right answer, and that is entirely the point. Read-the-room scenarios test whether your team can predict how others in the organisation would respond to real situations. And the debate rounds? That's where things get most revealing — and most memorable.

Every round is scored in real time on a live leaderboard, and your MC keeps the energy and pace sharp throughout. After the main game, there's a final round before the awards ceremony and a facilitated debrief that connects what just happened in the room to what actually happens at work every day.

This experience is both entertaining and genuinely revealing. The conversations that surface during the values rounds often continue long after the day is over — and that is exactly how it should be.

### FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: Does this experience require knowledge of our specific company values?**
Not necessarily. Culture Throwdown works with both explicit company values and the unwritten norms that shape how teams actually behave. We can tailor rounds to reference your organisation's stated values — just let your Team Connect contact know and we'll incorporate them.

**Q: What if some team members are reserved or don't like being put on the spot?**
The MC is skilled at creating an inclusive atmosphere where no one feels singled out. Participation happens at team level — you share answers, debate together, and present as a group. The format naturally draws quieter participants in without putting them on the spot individually.

**Q: Can the experience be customised to our company's culture or current priorities?**
Absolutely. We can incorporate your company's specific values, cultural themes, or even current strategic priorities into the rounds. The more context you share with us beforehand, the more tailored and meaningful the experience becomes.

**Q: Is this suitable for teams going through change or tension?**
Yes — in fact, it can be particularly valuable in those moments. The structured, game-format creates psychological safety for honest conversations that might otherwise be difficult to have. Our facilitators are experienced in managing these dynamics sensitively.

**Q: How competitive does it get? Will it create division?**
Healthy competition is built into the experience, but the facilitation is designed to keep the focus on exploration rather than winning at all costs. The debrief reframes the competitive moments as useful data about how your team thinks and collaborates.

### GETTING THE MOST FROM YOUR EXPERIENCE

**Encourage honest responses.** The most valuable moments in Culture Throwdown happen when people say what they actually think — not what they think they should say. Let your team know in advance that this is a safe space for real opinions.

**Mix your teams cross-functionally.** The experience is richest when people from different departments or levels are grouped together. It surfaces different perspectives on shared values and creates connection across the organisation.

**Take the debrief seriously.** The game is entertaining, but the debrief is where the real value is extracted. Block enough time for it and come in with a genuine curiosity about what the experience revealed.

**Follow up on the conversations.** When something surfaces in the room — a tension, a shared belief, a difference in perspective — use it as a starting point for real follow-up. Culture Throwdown is most powerful when the conversations don't end when the leaderboard does.
