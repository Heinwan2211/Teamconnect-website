# Facilitator Guide: Hybrid Challenge Series
### Modular Challenge Experience | In-Person and Remote Participants | 20–500 Participants | 2–3 Hours | Hybrid

---

## === SECTION 1: FACILITATOR PREPARATION GUIDE ===

### Experience Overview and Objectives

The Hybrid Challenge Series is a modular challenge experience designed to run simultaneously for in-person and remote participants. Digital challenges are delivered to remote teams via the shared platform, while in-person teams complete physical challenges in the room. Both streams feed into a single shared leaderboard, creating genuine competition and connection across locations. The experience is built for organisations with distributed workforces who want to create meaningful shared moments regardless of where people are.

**Primary objectives:**
- Bridge the gap between in-person and remote colleagues through shared competitive experience
- Build inclusion and connection across distributed teams
- Surface collaboration skills within and across locations
- Demonstrate that geography does not limit team performance
- Create memorable shared reference points across the organisation

### Ideal Group Size and Duration

- **Minimum:** 20 participants (10 in-person, 10 remote — or any combination)
- **Maximum:** 500 participants
- **Optimal group size:** 50–200 participants across locations
- **Team size:** 4–6 people per team (mixed in-person and remote strongly encouraged)
- **Total duration:** 2–3 hours depending on number of challenge rounds
  - Pre-briefing and tech check: 20 minutes
  - Challenge rounds: 90–120 minutes (5–8 rounds depending on format)
  - Break: 10 minutes
  - Leaderboard reveal and debrief: 30 minutes

### Venue and Space Requirements

**In-person location:**
- Open floor space or large function room for physical challenge stations
- Clear, unobstructed areas for team movement between challenge stations
- Large screen or projector displaying shared leaderboard and live video feed
- Stable internet connection (minimum 50 Mbps) for streaming and platform sync
- Wireless microphone and PA system for in-room announcements
- Multiple screens or monitors if facilitating challenge instructions from platform

**Remote participant requirements:**
- Reliable video conferencing platform (Zoom, Teams, or Google Meet — confirm with client)
- Stable individual internet connections (minimum 10 Mbps per remote participant)
- Webcam required for challenge verification
- Platform access link sent minimum 48 hours before event
- Remote tech host available throughout event (separate from main facilitator)

**Recommended in-person venue types:** corporate function rooms, hotel conference centres, open-plan office spaces cleared for the event.

### Equipment and Materials Checklist

**Platform and technology:**
- Challenge platform login credentials (confirm with platform provider 72 hours prior)
- Shared leaderboard software — test cross-location sync before event
- Streaming setup: webcam or PTZ camera for in-room feed visible to remote teams
- Backup internet connection (mobile hotspot minimum 4G)
- Screen mirroring or casting solution for leaderboard display
- Video conferencing licence for full participant count

**In-person physical challenge materials:**
- Challenge station kits (specific to chosen modules — provided by Team Connect)
- Timer display visible to all in-room participants
- Team name cards and table/station markers
- Printed team instructions for each challenge round (backup to digital)

**Remote challenge materials:**
- Digital challenge pack distributed via platform
- Downloadable assets sent to remote participants 24 hours before
- Verification instructions for remote challenge completion

### Setup Instructions (Step by Step)

1. Arrive at venue minimum 90 minutes before participant arrival.
2. Set up physical challenge stations — one per challenge module, clearly numbered and spaced.
3. Connect and test AV: projector, screen, PA system, streaming camera.
4. Log into challenge platform — confirm leaderboard displays correctly for in-room and test remote sync.
5. Connect video conferencing — confirm remote participants can see in-room screen and hear facilitator clearly.
6. Set up remote tech host workstation (laptop dedicated to managing remote participants' feed).
7. Test all physical challenge materials — ensure kits are complete and sealed.
8. Confirm remote participants have received platform links and access credentials.
9. Send 30-minute pre-event connection reminder to remote participants.
10. Brief in-room assistants: challenge station monitors, roving support, AV support.
11. Conduct live tech test with remote tech host 30 minutes before start.
12. 15 minutes before start: open video platform for early remote joiners and troubleshoot connections.

### Facilitator Briefing Notes

- Your role spans both rooms simultaneously — manage energy in the physical space while keeping remote participants visible and included.
- Narrate in-room activity for remote participants who cannot see the physical challenges directly.
- Treat remote teams as first-class participants — name them, acknowledge their scores, celebrate their wins verbally.
- Ensure the remote tech host is empowered to pause a round if remote teams experience connectivity issues.
- Mixed teams (in-person + remote members on the same team) require additional facilitation to prevent in-person members from dominating.
- Rotate challenge types so each round favours a different skill set — this prevents one location type from dominating all rounds.
- Keep the leaderboard visible and updated throughout — competitive tension maintains energy across both groups.

### Weather/Contingency Considerations

- **Remote connectivity failure:** Have a protocol for dropping to asynchronous challenge submission if a participant's connection drops. Do not restart entire rounds — credit the completed portion.
- **In-person AV failure:** Switch to facilitator-narrated score updates if leaderboard display fails; resume digital leaderboard as soon as restored.
- **Platform failure:** Revert to WhatsApp or email-based challenge submission with manual scoring as last resort.
- **Low remote engagement:** Have the remote tech host conduct a direct verbal check-in with each remote team between rounds.

### Pre-Event Client Communication Checklist

- [ ] Confirm in-person headcount and remote headcount separately
- [ ] Confirm video conferencing platform preference
- [ ] Send platform access instructions to all remote participants
- [ ] Confirm in-person venue AV capabilities
- [ ] Share participant information sheet (Section 5) for distribution
- [ ] Confirm team composition — mixed or location-based (recommend mixed)
- [ ] Confirm number of challenge rounds with client
- [ ] Confirm availability of remote tech host
- [ ] Test platform with 2–3 remote participants as pilot 48 hours before

## === SECTION 2: HOSTING GUIDE ===

### Welcome and Opening Script (Word for Word)

> *"Good morning / afternoon — welcome to the Hybrid Challenge Series, wherever you're joining from.*
>
> *To everyone in the room with me — hello. And to everyone on screen — you are just as much a part of this as anyone standing here. Today, geography is not a factor. Only performance counts.*
>
> *My name is [YOUR NAME] and I'll be your host across both spaces today. I also have [REMOTE HOST NAME] managing our virtual space, so if you're remote and you need anything, they're your person.*
>
> *Here's how this works. We have [NUMBER] challenge rounds ahead of us. Each round is a different challenge — some physical, some digital, some creative, some strategic. Every team competes in every round. Your scores combine on one shared leaderboard — live, visible to all of us, right now.*
>
> *You will be competing against teams in this room and teams on screen. That leaderboard doesn't care where you are. It only cares how your team performs.*
>
> *Ready to find out who the best team really is? Let's go."*

### Rules Explanation Script

> *"Before we start — the rules.*
>
> *One — every team completes every challenge. No rounds are optional.*
>
> *Two — in-person teams complete the physical version of each challenge at your assigned station. Remote teams complete the digital version simultaneously on the platform. Both count equally.*
>
> *Three — time limits apply to every round. When the timer ends, scores lock. Late submissions receive zero for that round.*
>
> *Four — each challenge has specific completion criteria. Read them carefully before you start. Partial completion earns partial points.*
>
> *Five — the leaderboard is live and visible to everyone throughout. Final scores are tallied after the last round.*
>
> *Six — you may not communicate challenge answers to other teams. You may communicate within your team freely.*
>
> *Questions? Good. Let's start Round 1."*

### Energy Management Tips Throughout the Experience

- **Pre-start:** Greet remote participants by name as they appear on screen — this signals inclusion before the event even begins.
- **During challenge rounds:** Narrate the energy in the room for remote viewers: *"The team in the front row has just found something — I can see it in their faces..."*
- **Between rounds:** Use the leaderboard as an energy driver — read out top 3 positions and make it dramatic.
- **Mid-event:** Check in directly with at least two remote teams by name each round to maintain their connection to the room.
- **Final round:** Build tension before announcing it: *"This is the last round. The leaderboard can change in the next [X] minutes. Don't count anyone out."*

### How to Handle Reluctant Participants

- Remote reluctance is common — assign the remote tech host to check in on quiet teams directly.
- For in-person reluctance, use the physical challenge as an equaliser: everyone can contribute to something hands-on.
- Frame contribution broadly: *"Research roles, logistics roles, communication roles — there is a job for every person on this team."*

### How to Manage Dominant Participants

- In mixed hybrid teams, watch for in-person voices drowning out remote members.
- Explicitly redirect: *"Let's hear from the team members on screen — what do they think?"*
- For dominant in-person individuals, assign remote team liaison as their role: *"Your job this round is to make sure your remote teammates have everything they need."*

### Timing Guide (Minute by Minute)

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| T-90 min | In-person venue setup complete |
| T-60 min | Platform and leaderboard test complete |
| T-30 min | Remote tech host at workstation; platform open for early joiners |
| T-15 min | All remote participants should be connected; troubleshoot issues |
| 00:00 | Welcome script — address both in-room and remote simultaneously |
| 00:10 | Rules explained; teams confirmed; leaderboard visible |
| 00:15 | Round 1 begins |
| Per round | Read leaderboard after each round; build tension before announcing |
| 90–120 min | Final round complete; 10-minute break |
| Break +10 min | Final leaderboard reveal and debrief begins |
| Debrief end | Close |

### Common Problems and How to Solve Them

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Remote participant connectivity drops mid-round | Note their score at dropout; credit proportional points; reconnect for next round |
| Leaderboard doesn't sync between locations | Remote tech host keeps manual score; reconcile during break |
| In-person team has an unfair advantage in physical challenge | Confirm digital equivalent was calibrated for equal difficulty pre-event |
| Remote teams feel disconnected after first few rounds | Remote tech host does direct verbal shout-out; facilitator names remote teams on main screen |
| Challenge platform goes down | Revert to manual submission via shared messaging channel; announce change calmly |
| Large time zone difference makes scheduling difficult | Confirm the event time was agreed with client to minimise antisocial hours for any location |

### Safety Considerations

- In-person physical challenges: ensure challenge stations are clear of tripping hazards and materials are safe for all participants.
- Confirm venue evacuation procedures with venue coordinator before the event.
- Remote participants: no physical safety concerns — confirm data privacy with platform provider.
- Have first aid contact available for in-person location.

## === SECTION 3: DEBRIEF GUIDE ===

### Why Debrief Matters for This Experience

The Hybrid Challenge Series creates a unique dynamic: teams that have never been in the same room achieved the same goal together. The debrief is the moment to make that visible. Participants often underestimate how much they relied on remote teammates during the event — or discover unexpected friction they hadn't named. This debrief should surface both the success of the hybrid format and the honest challenges it creates.

### Debrief Timing (When and How Long)

- Deliver after the final leaderboard reveal — let the competitive result land before reflection begins.
- Duration: **25–30 minutes**
- Format: Start with whole group (in-room and remote visible simultaneously), move to small team reflection (4 minutes), return to whole group for share-back.

### 10 Debrief Questions (From Surface to Deep)

1. *"What was the hardest part of this experience — the challenge itself, or working across the screen?"*
2. *"At what moment did your team feel most like a real team today — regardless of where people were?"*
3. *"How did your team communicate between in-person and remote members? Who made the effort, and who waited?"*
4. *"Was there a moment where the hybrid format gave your team an advantage? Or a disadvantage?"*
5. *"Who surprised you today — someone you underestimated, in-room or remote?"*
6. *"When you look at the leaderboard, what does it tell you about your team's performance under pressure?"*
7. *"What didn't work? Be honest. What made it harder than it needed to be?"*
8. *"How much of what you experienced today do you recognise from your daily work together?"*
9. *"If your team always included people in multiple locations, what would you need to change about how you work?"*
10. *"What one specific behaviour would make the biggest difference to how your hybrid team performs going forward?"*

### How to Connect the Experience to Workplace Behaviour

- The hybrid format is not just an event design choice — it mirrors how most modern teams actually work. The challenges that surfaced today are the same ones that appear in every hybrid meeting.
- Remote participants who felt invisible or overlooked have a valuable insight for their managers to hear.
- In-person participants who defaulted to making decisions without remote input may not have noticed — the debrief is the moment to make it conscious.
- Teams that thrived likely had an explicit norm about checking in across the connection — name that as a transferable practice.

### How to Close the Debrief Powerfully

> *"You just ran [NUMBER] challenge rounds across two (or more) physical spaces. And you did it as one team.*
>
> *That matters. Not because we made it easy — but because you figured it out under real conditions.*
>
> *The question is: do you do that at work? Do the people on screen feel as included as the people in the room? Do you make the same effort on a Tuesday morning that you made today?*
>
> *[Pause]*
>
> *I want you to leave today with one commitment. Not a value. A behaviour. Something you will actually do differently on your next hybrid call. Take 60 seconds. Write it down. That's your challenge for next week."*

### What to Leave the Group With

- A named behaviour commitment for their next hybrid interaction
- Recognition that hybrid teamwork is a skill, not just a tool problem
- Awareness of who on their team bridges the gap between in-person and remote — and how to support that person

#### Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need to install any software?**
Your event coordinator will confirm the platform requirements. Most challenges run in-browser. The video conferencing platform may require a downloaded app — check your link in advance.

**What if my internet connection is unreliable?**
Connect via ethernet if possible. If you experience issues, contact [REMOTE TECH HOST NAME] immediately — they will be managing the virtual space throughout the event.

**Will remote teams be at a disadvantage?**
The challenge mix is designed for equal competition across locations. Some rounds will favour remote teams; others will favour in-person. The leaderboard treats all submissions equally.

**What if I've never done online team building before?**
That's completely fine. The facilitator will guide you through everything. Your remote tech host is available for any technical questions throughout.

**Can I join from a mobile phone?**
We strongly recommend a laptop or desktop for the best experience. Mobile access may limit your ability to complete some challenges.
