A Message from Team Connect

Thank you for prioritising your team's wellbeing. In a world that moves fast and asks a lot of the people in it, choosing to pause and invest in mental and emotional health is one of the most courageous and caring things an organisation can do. We don't take that lightly. Our Mental & Corporate Wellness experience is built on evidence, delivered with genuine care, and designed to leave your team with real tools they can use long after the day is over. We are honoured to hold this space with you.

What to Expect on the Day

You and your team will arrive to a space that has been intentionally prepared to feel different — calmer, warmer, and more intentional than a typical office or conference room. The atmosphere matters here. Your Team Connect wellness facilitator will welcome everyone with an opening that sets the tone: honest, gentle, and genuinely supportive.

The experience is structured around five evidence-based stations that your team will rotate through in small groups of four to eight. The fynbos sensory walk invites you to slow down and reconnect with the natural world — using touch, smell, and sight to create a moment of genuine stillness. The stress-mapping workshop gives your team language and tools to name what they're carrying, and to understand it as information rather than weakness. The peer-coaching framework session teaches a simple, powerful structure for supporting each other — one your team can keep using back at work.

The digital detox creative station is a deliberate act of putting screens down and making something with your hands — paint, collage, or texture-based creation in a quiet, naturally lit space. And the breathwork and grounding practice closes the circuit, giving your team a direct, body-based experience of calm that they can return to whenever they need it.

The experience closes with a team resilience charter — a co-created document in which your team articulates its shared commitments to psychological health. This is not a corporate exercise. It is a genuine, meaningful conversation about how your team wants to take care of each other. The charter is yours to keep.

What Your Team Will Walk Away With

  • A personal set of evidence-based wellbeing tools they can use independently, starting tomorrow
  • A team resilience charter — a living document of your team's shared wellbeing commitments
  • A shared vocabulary around stress, resilience, and psychological safety that makes future conversations easier
  • The experience of having been seen and supported in a professional context — which builds lasting trust
  • Genuine psychological relief for teams carrying the weight of high pressure, burnout, or change

Practical Information

What to Wear

Comfortable, loose-fitting clothing is recommended — especially for the breathwork and grounding station, where participants may lie on yoga mats. Layering is helpful as some stations are better suited to cooler, quieter environments.

What to Bring

  • An open mind and the willingness to be present
  • A journal or notebook if you like to write — some participants find it useful
  • Comfortable socks for the breathwork station

What We Provide

  • Facilitation by a professional wellness facilitator throughout
  • All five station setups and materials
  • Fynbos specimens, essential oils, and sensory materials
  • Art and creative materials for the digital detox station
  • Yoga mats and comfort items for the breathwork station
  • Team resilience charter template and materials

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this suitable for a team that's under pressure but not in crisis?
Absolutely. In fact, the most valuable time to invest in wellbeing is before a team reaches crisis point. This experience is designed for teams who are functioning, working hard, and deserve a genuine investment in their sustainability — not only for teams who are struggling.
What if some team members are sceptical about wellness experiences?
Scepticism is entirely welcome. Our facilitators are skilled at holding space for participants across a wide range of openness levels — and have repeatedly seen sceptics become the most engaged participants by the third station. The experience earns its credibility through quality and depth, not cheerfulness.
Will personal or sensitive topics come up? How are those handled?
The experience creates space for honest conversation, but it is not a therapy session. Our facilitators are trained to hold boundaries carefully — encouraging genuine sharing without pushing anyone beyond what they're comfortable with. If a participant needs additional support, our facilitators are equipped to signpost appropriate resources.
Is the breathwork station mandatory?
Every station is designed to be a genuinely valuable part of the experience. That said, our facilitators always prioritise comfort and consent. No participant will be pressured into any activity that doesn't feel right for them.
Can we do the full-day version?
Yes. The full-day format extends each station to 50 minutes, includes an extended lunch break, and concludes with a more substantial team resilience charter workshop. It is a deeper, more immersive experience. Your Team Connect contact can advise on whether the half-day or full-day format is right for your group.

Getting the Most from Your Experience

Communicate clearly to your team beforehand. Let them know the day is about genuine investment in their wellbeing — not a checklist exercise, not mandatory sharing, not performance. The clearer you are, the safer they'll feel arriving.

Create the right physical environment. The atmosphere on the day matters enormously. Avoid fluorescent lighting, loud ambient noise, or a cluttered space. Ask your Team Connect contact for venue guidance — we'll help you set it up right.

Model participation at leadership level. When leaders show up fully — not to observe but to participate — it signals to everyone else that this matters and it's safe to take it seriously. Presence from the top changes the experience for the whole room.

Honour the charter. After the day, the team resilience charter shouldn't go into a drawer. Consider reviewing it at your next team meeting, posting it somewhere visible, or using it as a check-in tool. The charter is only as powerful as the commitment you bring to it.
