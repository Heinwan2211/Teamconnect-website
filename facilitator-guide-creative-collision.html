Facilitator Guide: Creative Collision

Design Challenge Experience | Shark Tank Meets Maker Lab | AI-Assisted Brainstorming | 3–4 Hours | Indoor

SECTION 1: FACILITATOR PREPARATION GUIDE

Experience Overview and Objectives

Creative Collision is a design challenge experience where teams receive a real-world brief, ideate, build a prototype solution using unconventional materials, and pitch to a panel of surprise "investors." Think Shark Tank meets maker lab. The experience is deliberately designed to break down the habits of safe, conventional thinking — using AI-assisted brainstorming prompts, time pressure, and unusual materials to force creativity.

Primary objectives:

  • Break down silo thinking by combining cross-functional or mixed-level teams
  • Build comfort with ambiguity, iteration, and rapid decision-making
  • Surface creative problem-solving styles and innovation leadership
  • Create a shared experience of making, pitching, and defending ideas
  • Demonstrate that innovation doesn't require the right background — it requires the right conditions

Ideal Group Size and Duration

  • Minimum: 12 participants (3 teams of 4) | Maximum: 120 participants
  • Optimal group size: 20–80 participants | Team size: 4–6 people per team
  • Total duration: 3–4 hours
    • Briefing and warm-up: 20 minutes | Ideation sprint: 20 minutes | Build phase: 60 minutes
    • Pitch preparation: 20 minutes | Pitches and investor Q&A: 45–60 minutes | Awards and debrief: 30 minutes

Venue and Space Requirements

  • Indoor only — materials are not suitable for outdoor/windy environments
  • Open-plan floor space for "maker stations" — each team needs approximately 4m x 2m
  • Long tables (trestle-style) per team to spread out materials
  • A dedicated pitch stage area — even if it's just a cleared zone at the front with a mic stand
  • Good lighting across all workstations
  • Projector or large screen for brief delivery and investor panel display

Equipment and Materials Checklist

Maker materials kit per team: Cardboard sheets, tape (masking, duct, double-sided), scissors, markers, sticky notes, string, wire, rubber bands, newspaper, fabric scraps, plastic cups, straws, paper clips, mystery "wildcard" material

Team Connect also provides: AI brainstorming prompt cards, timer display, pitch evaluation scorecards, investor "paddle" cards (optional), prize certificates, PA system and wireless microphone, laptop + HDMI

Facilitator Briefing Notes

  • The brief should be ambitious but achievable — test your brief on a colleague before the event.
  • Recommended AI prompts: "What would this solution look like if it were designed for a child?" / "What is the most counterintuitive way to solve this problem?" / "What would this look like in 50 years?"
  • The facilitator is a coach during the build phase, not a helper. Resist the urge to solve problems for teams.
  • The investor panel should be briefed to ask challenging but fair questions.
  • Set a hard time limit per pitch (3–4 minutes presentation + 2 minutes investor Q&A).
  • Craft knives should be kept at the facilitator station and issued on request only — not left at tables.

Pre-Event Client Communication Checklist

  • Confirm participant count and number of teams
  • Confirm whether brief should be customised to company/industry context
  • Confirm investor panel composition: external guests, internal execs, or facilitator?
  • Confirm whether participants will use slides/devices for pitch presentation
  • Confirm AV setup with venue (screen, projector, connectivity)
  • Send participant information sheet (Section 5)
  • Confirm prize/award preference

SECTION 2: HOSTING GUIDE

Welcome and Opening Script (Word for Word)

"Welcome to Creative Collision.

I'm going to make you a bold claim right now, and you can hold me to it: by the end of today, your team will have built something that didn't exist this morning. From nothing. Using things that have no business being part of a business solution.

You're going to receive a brief. You're going to have a limited amount of time to discuss it, build a prototype, and pitch your solution to a panel of investors — investors who will not be gentle.

Here's what I need you to let go of for the next [3/4] hours: the idea that there is a right answer. There isn't. There's your answer — the one your team has the courage to commit to, build, and defend.

You don't need experience. You don't need a design degree. You need each other.

The winning team won't necessarily have the most polished pitch. They'll have the most interesting idea and the best story about how they got there.

Let's begin."

Energy Management Tips

  • Briefing phase (0–25 min): Energy is curious and cautious. Walk around and observe.
  • Ideation sprint (25–45 min): Use upbeat music. Drop AI prompts at minutes 5, 12, and 18.
  • Build phase (45–105 min): Reduce music volume. Check in with teams at 30-minute and 50-minute marks. Warn all teams at 10 minutes remaining.
  • Pitches (125–185 min): Match the energy of each pitch. Celebrate investor questions as a sign of engagement.

SECTION 3: DEBRIEF GUIDE

Why Debrief Matters for This Experience

Creative Collision generates enormous energy and often produces surprising results. Without a debrief, these insights dissipate within 24 hours. The debrief converts the emotional experience of creating under pressure into transferable insight about innovation, collaboration, and risk-taking.

Debrief Timing

  • Debrief after the awards. Duration: 25–30 minutes
  • Format: Whole group, with a brief pair reflection moment in the middle.

10 Debrief Questions (From Surface to Deep)

  1. "What was the first idea your team came up with — and why didn't you go with it?"
  2. "When did your team's concept actually click into place? What happened in that moment?"
  3. "Who on your team surprised you today — and what did they do?"
  4. "What would you have done differently in the ideation sprint if you had 10 more minutes?"
  5. "How did your team make the decision to commit to one idea? Was it agreed, voted, or led by someone?"
  6. "Where did the AI prompts help, confuse, or unlock something unexpected?"
  7. "When did you feel most out of your comfort zone — and what did you do with that feeling?"
  8. "What does the way your team built and pitched today tell you about how you approach new challenges at work?"
  9. "What conditions does your team need to do its best creative work? Are those conditions present in your day-to-day environment?"
  10. "If your team had a 'Creative Collision' moment in your actual work — what would you build?"

How to Close the Debrief Powerfully

"What you did today is exactly what the best teams in the world do when they're faced with a problem that has no obvious solution: they showed up, they disagreed productively, they made something, and they stood behind it.

That is not a creative skill. That is a courage skill. And you already have it.

The question is: where in your work are you not using it? Where are you waiting for more information, more certainty, more permission — when actually what you need is to start building?

Take one idea from today — not a prototype. A behaviour. Something you'll try differently. Write it down.

That is your Creative Collision takeaway."

SECTION 4: PREPARATION CHECKLIST

2 Weeks Before

  • Confirm participant count and team configuration
  • Confirm brief: use stock brief or customise for client industry/context
  • Confirm investor panel composition and availability
  • Confirm venue layout: workstation space, pitch zone, AV
  • Order materials kits (check wildcard material availability)
  • Send participant information sheet to client
  • Confirm prize preference

1 Week Before

  • Finalise and print brief envelopes (one per team)
  • Prepare AI brainstorm prompt cards / deck
  • Build or refresh pitch scorecard template
  • Pack and inventory all materials kits — count per team
  • Brief investor panel on role and scoring criteria
  • Test AV setup: laptop, HDMI, PA, timer

Day of Event

  • Arrive 90 minutes before participants
  • Set up workstations: tables, materials kits (sealed), timer
  • Set up pitch zone: microphone, projector/screen, investor seating and scorecards
  • Test AV and PA
  • Brief investors on their arrival
  • Welcome participants and begin on time
