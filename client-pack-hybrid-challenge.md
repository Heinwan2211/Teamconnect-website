### WHAT TO EXPECT ON THE DAY

Wherever your team is physically located on the day, the experience begins at the same time for everyone — with a joint welcome and tech check facilitated by your Team Connect host. In-person participants will gather at your venue; remote participants will join via the shared digital platform. Teams are deliberately mixed — in-person and remote participants grouped together — so that every team is genuinely hybrid from the first round.

The experience runs through five to eight challenge rounds over 90 to 120 minutes. In-person teams tackle physical challenges in the room — building tasks, coordination challenges, and creative team activities. Remote teams complete digital equivalents on the shared platform at the same time. Both streams feed into a single, shared leaderboard that everyone can see in real time. The points are real, the competition is real, and the connection is real.

Between rounds, your Team Connect host keeps the energy up and creates moments of cross-location interaction — remote teams can see what in-person teams are doing, and vice versa. These visibility moments are deliberately designed to dissolve the "out of sight, out of mind" dynamic that undermines so many distributed teams.

A short break at the midpoint keeps the energy fresh. The experience closes with a leaderboard reveal — with all teams, in-person and remote, celebrating the results together — followed by a facilitated debrief that draws out what the experience revealed about how your distributed team actually works.

### FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: What if the remote participants have unreliable internet?**
We conduct a pre-event tech check for all remote participants and provide platform support on the day. The experience is designed to be resilient to minor connectivity interruptions — remote participants can re-join rounds without losing their team's progress.

**Q: Do in-person and remote teams compete against each other?**
All teams — regardless of location — compete on the same shared leaderboard. In-person and remote participants are grouped together within teams, so no one is disadvantaged by their location. The scoring is calibrated to ensure fairness across challenge types.

**Q: Can we run this experience if most participants are remote and only a few are in-person?**
Yes. The experience works across any combination of in-person and remote — from fully remote to fully in-person and everything in between. We'll advise on the best format and team structure for your specific split.

**Q: How many people can participate?**
The Hybrid Challenge Series accommodates between 20 and 500 participants. Your Team Connect contact will advise on optimal team sizes and logistics based on your participant numbers.

**Q: Can we customise the challenges to our industry or company culture?**
Yes. Challenge rounds can be tailored to reflect your company's values, industry, or internal culture. Let your Team Connect contact know your preferences and we'll incorporate them into the design.

### GETTING THE MOST FROM YOUR EXPERIENCE

**Make the tech check non-negotiable.** Ensure all remote participants complete the pre-event tech check — it prevents the kind of connectivity issues that can disrupt momentum on the day. Your Team Connect contact will manage this process.

**Group people deliberately.** The most powerful team compositions mix in-person and remote participants from different departments and levels. Avoid grouping people who already work closely together — this is an opportunity to build new connections.

**Encourage the in-person group not to default inward.** It's natural for in-person participants to focus on each other and forget their remote teammates are watching. Brief your in-person team ahead of time to actively include and acknowledge their remote colleagues throughout.

**Use the debrief to surface the real insights.** After the leaderboard reveal, the debrief is where the experience becomes genuinely useful. Ask: what did we notice about how we communicated across locations? What worked? What can we do differently in our day-to-day collaboration?
