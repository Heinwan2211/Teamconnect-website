### WHAT TO EXPECT ON THE DAY

You and your team will gather at the starting point for a pre-race briefing that sets out the rules, the course structure, and the team composition. The energy is already high by the time your Team Connect race director takes the stage — this is a competitive event, and your team will feel it from the opening brief.

Teams of four to eight participants race between a series of checkpoints across the outdoor course. The race is not a simple run — every checkpoint presents a different challenge. Navigation checkpoints require your team to use map references, compass directions, or landmark clues to find the next location without a pre-marked route. Challenge checkpoints demand a specific physical or cognitive task before you receive your next clue. Code checkpoints present a cipher or puzzle that must be solved to unlock the next leg of the race. Speed checkpoints reward the first team to arrive with a time advantage.

Strategy matters as much as pace. Teams that communicate well, divide responsibilities effectively, and make quick, smart decisions will consistently outperform faster but less coordinated groups. This is a race that rewards teamwork — genuinely.

After the active race phase, all teams return to a central point for the debrief and awards ceremony. The leaderboard reveals the final results, but the debrief is where the real conversation happens — connecting what your team did on the course to how they work together every day.

### PRACTICAL INFORMATION

#### What to Wear

Comfortable activewear or sport casual is essential. Closed-toe, supportive shoes are mandatory — trail shoes or trainers are ideal. Dress for the weather; bring a light layer in cooler months and sun protection in summer. The race will continue in light rain — dress accordingly.

#### What to Bring

* Comfortable, appropriate footwear — this is the most important item on this list
* A fully charged mobile phone (required for the experience)
* Water bottle — participants will be active and outdoors for extended periods
* Sunscreen and a hat for sunny days
* A competitive spirit and a team-first attitude

### FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: How physically demanding is the Amazing Race?**
The experience involves sustained outdoor walking and light running over 90–150 minutes, depending on your group size and course design. Participants should be comfortable with moderate physical activity. The race is won through strategy and teamwork, not pure fitness — but a baseline of physical ability is required. Please flag any health concerns with your Team Connect contact before booking.

**Q: How are teams assigned?**
Teams are assigned by your Team Connect race director and can be structured to reflect cross-functional groupings, mixed experience levels, or any composition you choose. We'll discuss the optimal team composition strategy for your group during the planning process.

**Q: What happens if a team gets lost?**
Each team has a dedicated mobile contact number for the Team Connect race team. Checkpoint staff are also positioned at intervals across the course. No team will be left without support — your race director will always know where teams are and can assist if needed.

**Q: Can the race course be set in a specific location?**
The course design is adapted to your chosen location. We work with a range of outdoor environments across South Africa and will design the course to make the best use of the terrain, landmarks, and infrastructure available at your preferred location. Contact your Team Connect representative to discuss location options.

**Q: What happens at the checkpoint challenges — do all team members need to complete them?**
Checkpoint challenges are designed for teams, not individuals. The specific rules for each challenge will be briefed before the race begins. In most cases, the team works together to complete the challenge, and no single team member carries an unfair burden.

### GETTING THE MOST FROM YOUR EXPERIENCE

**Communicate the course format clearly beforehand.** Let participants know they'll be walking and running outdoors for up to 2–4 hours. The right footwear and clothing make a significant difference to how much people enjoy the experience — don't let an unprepared participant have a harder day than necessary.

**Brief your team on strategy, not just pace.** Teams that go out hard and exhaust themselves early rarely win. The teams that win communicate constantly, delegate checkpoint tasks clearly, and make good navigation decisions quickly. Share this before the race begins.

**Use the debrief to extract real insight.** The race reveals a lot about how your team actually operates under pressure. The debrief is where those observations become useful. Encourage your team to be honest about what they noticed — about themselves and each other.

**Celebrate the effort, not just the result.** The leaderboard matters, but so does the way your team raced. Acknowledge the moments of excellent teamwork, creative problem-solving, and leadership that happened across the course — regardless of finishing position.
