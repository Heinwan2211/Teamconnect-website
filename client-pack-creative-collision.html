What to Expect on the Day

You and your team will arrive to a room that looks a little like an inventor's workshop — tables covered in unconventional materials, whiteboards ready, and a facilitator who looks like they're up to something interesting. Creative Collision is a design challenge experience that draws on the best of Shark Tank and the maker lab — and it's more revealing than most people expect.

After a warm welcome and brief warm-up, you'll be divided into mixed teams of four to six. Each team receives a real-world brief — a genuine challenge with no obvious answer. You'll go through a structured ideation sprint using AI-assisted brainstorming prompts, then move into the build phase where you'll construct a working prototype using whatever unconventional materials are on your table. The constraint is deliberate: it forces creative thinking rather than conventional problem-solving.

After the build phase, each team prepares their pitch. Then comes the moment of truth — you'll present your solution to a panel of surprise "investors" who will probe, question, and ultimately decide which team has created the most compelling solution. The pitches are energetic, sometimes hilarious, and always illuminating.

The debrief at the end of the day draws out what the experience revealed about your team's innovation style, creative confidence, and collaborative decision-making — and connects those insights to how you can work better together back at the office.

What Your Team Will Walk Away With

  • Greater creative confidence — the direct experience of building and defending an original idea under pressure
  • A toolkit of practical ideation techniques that can be applied in real work contexts
  • Deeper understanding of how different thinking styles complement each other in a team
  • The ability to pitch and defend ideas more clearly and persuasively
  • A shared memory of having made something together — which creates real team cohesion

Practical Information

Smart casual and comfortable. You'll be on your feet during the build phase, so comfortable shoes are recommended. Don't wear anything you'd mind getting a bit creative with — occasional use of glue, paint, or craft materials is part of the experience.

What to Bring

  • Willingness to look a little ridiculous in the name of creativity
  • No preparation required — in fact, not preparing is part of the design

Frequently Asked Questions

What if we're not a creative team?
That's exactly why this experience works. Creative Collision is designed for people who don't think of themselves as creative — and proves that the right conditions unlock creativity in everyone. The structure, the time pressure, and the unusual materials do the heavy lifting.
Who are the "investors" on the panel?
The investor panel is typically made up of senior leaders from your organisation, external guests, or Team Connect facilitators playing the role. We'll discuss the best composition with you beforehand to ensure the right level of challenge and relevance.
Can we customise the brief to our industry or company context?
Absolutely. We can tailor the design brief to reflect your industry, your current business challenges, or your company's strategic priorities. The more context you give us, the more relevant and impactful the experience becomes.
Will quieter or more introverted team members be included?
Yes. Creative Collision is structured to value multiple types of contribution — ideation, building, research, and presenting are all distinct roles within the team. Quieter participants often thrive in the build phase, and the structured format ensures no one is left without a meaningful role.
Is this suitable for senior leadership teams?
Absolutely. In fact, senior leadership teams often get the most from this experience because it puts them in an unfamiliar, unscripted environment. The experience surfaces authentic leadership behaviours and creates rich debrief material for high-performing teams.

Getting the Most from Your Experience

Brief your team on what to expect — but not too much. Let them know the day will involve a creative challenge and a pitch, but don't over-prepare them. The slight discomfort of not knowing exactly what's coming is part of what makes the experience powerful.

Celebrate the unexpected. When someone on your team surprises you — with an idea, a contribution, or a skill you didn't know they had — make sure they know you noticed. Creative Collision regularly reveals hidden strengths, and that recognition matters.

Use the debrief to connect insights to real work. Ask your team: what does this tell us about how we approach problems at work? How could we bring more of this creative energy into our actual projects? The debrief is where the experience becomes lasting change.

Follow up with a creative challenge at work. In the week following the experience, give your team a small, low-stakes creative brief and see what changes. One of the most powerful things Creative Collision does is give people permission to think differently — don't let that permission expire.
