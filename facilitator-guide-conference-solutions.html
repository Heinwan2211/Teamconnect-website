Conference Activation Experiences | 6 Formats | 15–90 Minutes Each | Indoor | Any Conference Size

Section 1: Facilitator Preparation Guide

Experience Overview and Objectives

Conference Solutions are a suite of six activation formats designed to be deployed at specific points within a conference schedule. Each format serves a different conference need — opening energy, post-lunch recovery, cross-table networking, values integration, creative stimulation, or closing synthesis. They range from 15 to 90 minutes and can be used individually or in combination across a multi-day conference. The facilitator's role is to read the conference's energy precisely and deliver the right format at the right moment, in a way that feels seamlessly integrated into the event rather than bolted on.

Primary objectives:

  • Maintain participant energy and engagement across a long conference day
  • Create connection between delegates who may not know each other
  • Reinforce conference themes, values, or key messages through experience
  • Provide active, participatory moments that offset passive listening
  • Support the conference arc — opening energy, sustained engagement, powerful close

The 6 Conference Solution Formats

FORMAT 1: MORNING IGNITION

Duration: 15–30 minutes | Position: Conference opening or post-registration

Purpose: Energise the room, create connection, and set the tone for the day before the first speaker.

Format description: A rapid, high-energy sequence of short physical and social activities designed to wake up the room, introduce participants to their tablemates, and signal that this conference will be active and participatory. Activities are designed to be completed at delegates' seats or with minimal movement.

Typical activity sequence:

  1. Brief physical energiser — 3 minutes (standing stretch, breathing reset, or physical warm-up)
  2. Table introduction activity — 5 minutes (structured introduction: "Tell your table your name, your role, and the one thing you most want from today.")
  3. Cross-table connector — 5 minutes (one shared question to the whole room; hands up, quick poll, brief responses from floor)
  4. Thematic primer — 5 minutes (a question that primes the day's theme: "In one word — what does [CONFERENCE THEME] mean to you?")
  5. Close and hand-off to first speaker

Facilitator notes:

  • Do not start until the room is at minimum 80% seated. A half-empty room will not generate the energy required.
  • Use music during setup and as participants settle — cut music as you begin.
  • Be specific with table activity instructions — vague prompts produce quiet tables.
  • Hand off to the first speaker with energy: "And now — to open today officially — please welcome [SPEAKER NAME]."

FORMAT 2: POST-LUNCH RE-ENERGISER

Duration: 15–20 minutes | Position: Immediately after lunch, before afternoon content

Purpose: Break the post-lunch energy dip and re-engage participants before afternoon sessions.

Format description: A physical and mental reset designed to counteract the physiological drop in energy that follows a meal. Combines light physical movement with a short cognitive challenge and a social interaction.

Typical activity sequence:

  1. Physical reset — 5 minutes (guided, standing: stretch, movement, or brief exercise sequence)
  2. Quick cognitive challenge — 5 minutes (team puzzle, word problem, or rapid-fire quiz at tables)
  3. Energy call — 3 minutes (direct whole-room engagement: audience polls, group decisions, show of hands)
  4. Close and transition to afternoon speaker

Facilitator notes:

  • Begin the moment scheduled — do not wait for stragglers. Latecomers re-enter into an already-energised room.
  • Physical movement must be accessible to all ability levels. Offer seated alternatives.
  • Keep the cognitive challenge simple and fast — this is not a learning activity, it is an energy trigger.
  • Use your voice dynamically — loud, upbeat, and clear. This is not the moment for calm.

FORMAT 3: CROSS-TABLE CONNECTOR

Duration: 20–30 minutes | Position: Mid-morning or mid-afternoon; between content sessions

Purpose: Create connections between participants who do not know each other; break conference silos.

Format description: A structured networking activity using table rotation and guided conversation prompts. Participants rotate between tables, meeting new delegates and engaging in purposeful conversations.

Typical activity sequence:

  1. Brief framing — 2 minutes (explain the purpose: connection across the room, not small talk)
  2. Round 1: table prompt conversation — 7 minutes (each table has a conversation starter tied to conference theme)
  3. Rotation — 2 minutes (participants move to a new table following a numbered or colour-coded system)
  4. Round 2: new prompt, new table — 7 minutes
  5. Optional Round 3 if time allows — 7 minutes
  6. Whole-room share-back — 3 minutes (two or three key insights from the conversations)

Facilitator notes:

  • Assign rotation clearly in advance. Unstructured movement creates chaos.
  • Table prompts should be relevant to the conference theme — not generic icebreaker questions.
  • Time each round with a visible countdown and audio cue for rotation.

FORMAT 4: VALUES IN ACTION

Duration: 30–45 minutes | Position: Mid-conference; often day 2 of multi-day events

Purpose: Bring organisational values to life through active exploration and team challenge.

Format description: A table-based activity where teams explore, discuss, and demonstrate a specific company value through a structured challenge. Each table receives a value and a challenge brief — they must create a short (2-minute) live demonstration, skit, or presentation that shows the value in action.

Typical activity sequence:

  1. Values introduction — 5 minutes (facilitator introduces the company's values framework)
  2. Table challenge brief — 5 minutes (each table receives their value and brief; confirm rules)
  3. Table preparation time — 15–20 minutes (teams develop their demonstration)
  4. Presentations — 2 minutes per table (all tables present to the room)
  5. Audience vote — 2 minutes (quick show of hands or app-based poll)
  6. Debrief — 5 minutes (brief facilitator reflection on what the presentations revealed)

Facilitator notes:

  • Confirm the values and challenge brief with the client before the event. The values must be the company's actual values.
  • Encourage creativity: skits, presentations, visual demonstrations, and songs are all welcome.
  • Keep presentation time strict at 2 minutes — use a visible timer on screen.

FORMAT 5: CREATIVE SPARK

Duration: 30–45 minutes | Position: Mid-conference; ideal for breaking up heavy content days

Purpose: Unlock creative thinking and break pattern-based thinking through a structured creative challenge.

Format description: Teams tackle a creative challenge that has no single correct answer. Challenges are designed to surface divergent thinking and demonstrate that creativity is a team skill, not an individual talent.

Typical activity sequence:

  1. Creative framing — 3 minutes (brief on why creativity matters for this conference / organisation)
  2. Challenge brief — 2 minutes (specific challenge delivered to all teams simultaneously)
  3. Creation time — 20–25 minutes (teams work to complete the creative challenge using provided materials)
  4. Presentations — 2 minutes per team
  5. Audience appreciation — explicit acknowledgement of creative risk-taking (not a competitive vote)
  6. Facilitated reflection — 5 minutes (what surprised you? What assumptions did you break?)

Materials required (provide per table): A3 paper or flip chart page, markers (multiple colours), sticky notes. Optional: LEGO, craft materials, magazines for cut-and-paste if budget allows.

Facilitator notes:

  • Explicitly remove "right answer" pressure at the start: "There is no correct response. We are looking for interesting, unexpected, or brave."
  • Encourage teams to commit to their idea rather than second-guessing.

FORMAT 6: CONFERENCE CLOSER

Duration: 30–60 minutes | Position: Final session of the conference

Purpose: Synthesise the conference's key insights, create shared commitments, and send delegates away with energy and clarity.

Format description: A structured synthesis and commitment activity that converts the conference's content into personal and team actions.

Typical activity sequence:

  1. Individual reflection — 5 minutes (each participant writes answers to 3 prompt questions)
  2. Table discussion — 10 minutes (share reflections; identify 2–3 themes that emerged at the table)
  3. Table commitment — 5 minutes (each table agrees on one collective commitment for the next 30 days)
  4. Whole-room share-back — 10 minutes (each table shares their commitment; facilitator captures on screen or flip chart)
  5. Individual commitment card — 3 minutes (participants write their personal commitment; fold and keep)
  6. Facilitator close — 5 minutes (synthesis of the room's commitments; connection to conference theme)

Reflection prompt questions (adaptable):

  • "What is the single most important thing you learned or were reminded of at this conference?"
  • "What will you do differently in your work as a result of being here?"
  • "Who do you need to share this insight with when you return?"

Facilitator notes:

  • The tone shifts here: slower, more reflective, more personal. Do not maintain high energy into a close.
  • Give participants genuine silence for the individual reflection. Do not fill it.
  • The collective table commitments should be specific and realistic — redirect vague commitments: "'Be more collaborative' is a value, not a commitment. What specifically will you do?"

General Venue and Space Requirements (All Formats)

  • Tables with 4–8 delegates per table (round or rectangular both work)
  • Stage or raised area with microphone for facilitator
  • Large screen or projector for timing, prompts, and presentations
  • Sound system for music cues and announcements
  • Adequate lighting for reading and writing activities
  • Space between tables for movement (Cross-Table Connector requires rotation space)

General Equipment Checklist

  • Wireless microphone and PA system
  • Laptop and HDMI adapter
  • Timer displayed on screen (large, visible countdown)
  • Presentation slides for each format's instructions and prompts
  • Printed activity materials per table (confirm per format)
  • Markers and paper at each table (standard for all formats)
  • Music playlist per format (Morning Ignition: upbeat; Closer: calm and warm)

Pre-Event Client Communication Checklist

  • Confirm which Conference Solution formats are being used and when in the programme
  • Confirm conference theme and values to be integrated
  • Share participant information (Section 5) with conference organiser
  • Confirm table configuration and delegate numbers
  • Confirm AV requirements with venue
  • Confirm any printed materials required and delivery deadline
  • Confirm facilitator arrival time and access to conference space for setup

Section 2: Hosting Guide

General Welcome Framework (Adaptable Per Format)

"[CONFERENCE NAME] has [X] minutes that are yours — not for presentations, not for slides, but for you and the people at your table.

What we're about to do is designed to [FORMAT PURPOSE: connect you / energise you / synthesise the day / bring your values to life].

Here's what's going to happen: [BRIEF FORMAT DESCRIPTION]. You have everything you need at your table.

Here's how it works: [INSTRUCTIONS]. Any questions? Good. Let's go."

Common Problems and How to Solve Them

ProblemSolution

Conference Closer reflection falls flatAsk a specific person at a specific table to share their response; this primes the room

Section 3: Debrief Guide

Debrief Approach for Conference Solutions

Each Conference Solution format has its own debrief embedded in the session design. The debrief questions below are general starting points; facilitators should adapt them to the specific format and conference content.

Format-Specific Debrief Questions

Morning Ignition:

  1. "What's one thing about someone at your table that you wouldn't have known if we hadn't done that?"

Post-Lunch Re-Energiser:

  1. "Your brain just did something different for 15 minutes. What does it feel like to switch modes mid-day?"

Cross-Table Connector:

  1. "Who did you meet today that you want to continue the conversation with? What were you talking about?"
  2. "What did you learn from someone at another table that would have been useful to you before today?"

Values in Action:

  1. "Which demonstration of [VALUE] most surprised you — and why?"
  2. "When you see your value lived well in your actual work, what does it look like? Name a specific example."

Creative Spark:

  1. "When was the last time you were creative at work — really creative, not just problem-solving? What made it possible?"
  2. "What assumption did your team break during that challenge?"

Conference Closer: See Section 1, Format 6 — the full debrief is built into the format.

For all formats, the close follows the same principle: connect the activity to the conference's purpose, and leave participants with one thing to carry forward.

Section 4: Preparation Checklist

2 Weeks Before

  • Confirm which formats are being delivered and at what times in the conference programme
  • Confirm conference theme and any values or messages to integrate
  • Design or adapt activity content for each format
  • Confirm printed materials and order/print per table count
  • Confirm AV requirements with conference venue or AV supplier
  • Brief co-facilitators if multiple formats across a multi-day event

1 Week Before

  • Finalise presentation slides for all formats
  • Test slides on conference AV system (or confirm compatibility)
  • Prepare all printed materials and table packs
  • Prepare music playlists per format
  • Confirm conference programme timing — confirm buffer time before and after each format
  • Confirm facilitator access to conference space for setup

Day Before

  • Pack facilitator kit: laptop, HDMI, adapters, microphone, materials
  • Confirm delivery of printed materials to venue
  • Review final conference programme for any timing changes

Day of Event

  • Arrive before delegates for the first format in which you facilitate
  • Confirm AV and slide display before delegates enter
  • Set up table materials for each format before its scheduled time
  • Coordinate with conference AV operator on music cues
  • Deliver each format to schedule
  • Debrief with conference organiser after final session

Your Conference Experience — What to Expect

Your conference includes interactive sessions designed to energise, connect, and inspire you. Here's what you need to know.

About the Interactive Sessions

Throughout the conference, there will be [NUMBER] facilitated interactive sessions. These are not additional presentations — they are active, participatory experiences designed to help you get more from the conference, connect with your colleagues, and bring the conference themes to life.

Formats may include:

  • Energisers and warm-up activities
  • Structured networking and conversation
  • Creative team challenges
  • Values-based activities
  • Synthesis and commitment sessions

What to Expect

  • Your facilitator will guide you through each session clearly.
  • Some sessions take place at your table; others involve brief movement around the room.
  • All activities are designed for professional conference environments — no extreme physical activity, no embarrassing participation.
  • You will always have everything you need at your table.

What to Bring

  • A pen and notebook (some sessions involve written reflection)
  • An open mind — these sessions work best when you participate fully
