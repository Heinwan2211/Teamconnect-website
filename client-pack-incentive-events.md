### FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: How large a group can you accommodate?**
Incentive & Fun Events scale from 20 to over 1,000 participants. Larger events require a full production team and additional lead time — please contact us as early as possible if your group exceeds 300 people.

**Q: Can we include individual recognition and awards?**
Absolutely. Recognition moments — spotlight awards, top performer acknowledgements, milestone celebrations — can be built into any Incentive & Fun Event format. Share your recognition criteria and recipients with your Team Connect contact during planning.

**Q: Can this experience be themed?**
Yes. Themed events are one of our favourite formats to design. Whether it's a specific decade, a movie theme, a sports theme, or your company's own culture and identity, we can build the experience around a theme that resonates with your team.

**Q: How much planning time do you need?**
Standard Incentive & Fun Events require a minimum of four to six weeks of planning time. Large-scale or highly customised events require eight to twelve weeks. Contact us as early as possible to secure your preferred date and allow sufficient planning time.

**Q: Can you help with venue selection?**
Yes. We work with a network of venues across South Africa and can recommend options that suit your group size, format, budget, and location. We can also bring our production to your preferred venue if you have one in mind.

### GETTING THE MOST FROM YOUR EXPERIENCE

**Invest in pre-event communication.** Build anticipation by communicating the event to your team in advance. The more excited people arrive, the more energy there is to work with on the day. Your Team Connect contact can provide communication templates.

**Get leadership visible and present.** When senior leaders are genuinely present and participating — not watching from the edges — it signals to the entire team that this day matters. That presence amplifies the feeling of recognition.

**Personalise the recognition moments.** Generic awards feel generic. When recognition is specific — naming the exact contribution, the specific project, the particular quality that made a difference — it lands with genuine weight. Brief your speakers accordingly.

**End on a high.** The close of the event is the last thing your team will carry home. Make sure it's intentional — a final moment of energy, connection, or recognition that sends everyone out on the right note. Your Team Connect facilitator will help you design this closing beat.
