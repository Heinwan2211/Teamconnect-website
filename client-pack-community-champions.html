A Message from Team Connect

Thank you for choosing Community Champions — and for choosing to do something that matters. This experience is close to our hearts because it connects two things we believe in deeply: building great teams and making a genuine difference in our communities. Your team is going to work hard, laugh together, and leave feeling proud of what they built — not just as a team, but as people. We are honoured to be part of that.

What to Expect on the Day

You and your team will arrive to a workspace already set up with everything you need to get building — materials, tools, and a clear sense of purpose. After a welcome and brief from your Team Connect facilitator, you'll be divided into teams of five to ten and invited to choose your impact track for the day.

Impact tracks are the hands-on community projects your teams will complete. Options include assembling food garden kits for local schools, building habitat structures for wildlife, or preparing care packages for shelter residents. Each track involves real making — measuring, assembling, packing, and crafting — alongside creative problem-solving and genuine teamwork. Quality, teamwork, and creativity are all scored in real time on our impact scoring system.

Throughout the experience, your Team Connect facilitator will keep the energy up, the competition friendly, and the purpose front and centre. This isn't a standard craft afternoon — it's meaningful work with visible outcomes, and your team will feel the difference.

The experience closes with our handover ceremony: representatives from the beneficiary organisations join you on the day to receive the completed projects. This moment — when the work your team has done is handed to someone who genuinely needs it — is consistently the most emotionally resonant moment of any experience we run. Bring a tissue.

What Your Team Will Walk Away With

  • A genuine sense of shared purpose — the feeling of having built something together that matters beyond the office
  • Stronger team cohesion built through hands-on, collaborative, outcome-driven work
  • A documentable CSR contribution that reflects your organisation's values in action
  • Connection across teams and levels through a shared experience of giving
  • Pride — the lasting, specific kind that comes from knowing your team made a real difference

Practical Information

What to Wear

Casual and comfortable — you'll be doing hands-on work. Closed-toe shoes are recommended. Older clothes are fine; we'll be working with materials that might get a little messy.

What to Bring

  • Work gloves if you prefer them (we'll have some available too)
  • A generous spirit and a competitive streak — both will serve you well
  • Camera or phone for photos — this is a day worth documenting

What We Provide

  • All project materials for your chosen impact track
  • Team Connect facilitation team throughout
  • Real-time impact scoring display
  • Awards and recognition for the highest-impact team
  • Coordination of the handover ceremony, including beneficiary representative attendance

Dietary & Accessibility

Please inform us of any dietary or accessibility needs at least one week before the event by emailing Info@teamconnect.co.za. Community Champions is designed to be inclusive — all impact tracks have options that accommodate different physical abilities.

Parking & Arrival

Please plan to arrive 10–15 minutes before the start time. Full venue details, including parking guidance, will be shared separately once your venue is confirmed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who are the beneficiary organisations?
We work with a rotating selection of verified community organisations including schools, shelters, and environmental conservation partners. Your Team Connect contact will confirm which organisations will be involved in your specific event.
Can we choose which impact track we work on as a team?
Teams are guided through the track selection process at the start of the experience. We typically structure selection so that different teams take on different tracks, creating variety and breadth of impact across the day. Your Team Connect contact can advise on how to handle this for your specific group.
Is this suitable for large groups?
Community Champions scales beautifully for large groups — up to 300 participants — by running multiple impact tracks simultaneously. We'll design the logistics to ensure every team has a clear, meaningful task regardless of group size.
Can we use this experience to fulfil our CSR commitments?
Absolutely. Community Champions produces documentable impact — quantities of items completed, organisations supported, and estimated community value generated. We can provide a post-event impact report for your CSR and ESG reporting purposes. Please ask your Team Connect contact for details.
Will representatives from the beneficiary organisations really be there?
Yes. The handover ceremony with live beneficiary representation is a non-negotiable part of the Community Champions experience. It's what gives the day its emotional resonance, and we manage all logistics to make it happen.

Getting the Most from Your Experience

Brief your team on the purpose. Let them know in advance that this isn't just a team building activity — it's a genuine community contribution. Teams who arrive understanding the stakes put more care into their work and get more out of the experience.

Mix up your teams. Cross-functional, cross-level groupings produce the richest dynamics. When a senior leader and a junior team member are building something together with their hands, hierarchy dissolves and real connection happens.

Encourage craftsmanship, not just speed. The impact scoring rewards quality and creativity, not just quantity. Teams that rush sometimes produce less impact than those who take care with their work. Brief your team accordingly.

Stay for the handover ceremony. This is the moment the day becomes something more than memorable — it becomes meaningful. Ensure your team is present and present in the moment.
