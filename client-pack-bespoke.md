### WHAT TO EXPECT ON THE DAY

Your Bespoke Experience is, by definition, yours — so the specifics of the day will reflect the design we develop together during the discovery process. What we can tell you is this: whatever we build, it will be deliberately crafted to feel like it was made for your team. Because it was.

You and your team will arrive to an experience that is fully set up and ready for you. Your Team Connect facilitation team will have spent significant time preparing — not just logistics, but the details that make a bespoke experience feel genuinely special. The welcome, the framing, the activities, and the close will all be aligned to your objectives, your culture, and your team's current moment.

Depending on your design, the day may include competitive elements, collaborative challenges, creative activities, reflective workshops, outdoor exploration, or a combination. It may be high-energy and playful, or quiet and contemplative. It may bring different departments together, celebrate a milestone, address a named cultural challenge, or support your team through a period of change. What it will always be is purposeful, professional, and personal.

The debrief and close will be designed with the same care as everything else — ensuring your team leaves with clarity, connection, and something genuinely worth taking back to work.

### FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: How does the discovery process work?**
Your Team Connect experience designer will schedule one or two conversations with you — typically a kick-off call and a design review — to understand your team, your objectives, and your constraints. We'll ask questions about your culture, your current challenges, what's worked before, and what hasn't. From there, we build your experience design and share it for your input before finalising.

**Q: How much lead time do you need for a bespoke design?**
We recommend a minimum of four to six weeks for a quality bespoke design. More complex experiences — those involving custom materials, unique venues, or multi-format programmes — may require eight to twelve weeks. Please contact us as early as possible to secure your preferred date.

**Q: Can we incorporate our company branding or specific values into the experience?**
Absolutely. Bespoke experiences are designed to reflect your identity — including your brand, your values, your language, and your culture. Just share the relevant materials with your Team Connect contact during the discovery process.

**Q: What if we're not sure exactly what we need?**
That's exactly what the discovery process is for. You don't need to arrive with a clear brief — you just need to be willing to have an honest conversation about where your team is and where you want them to be. We'll help you get clear on the design from there.

**Q: Can a bespoke experience include elements from your other programmes?**
Yes. Many of our best bespoke designs draw on elements from across the Team Connect portfolio — a challenge format here, a reflection structure there — combined into something entirely tailored. We use our existing toolkit to build something new.

### GETTING THE MOST FROM YOUR EXPERIENCE

**Invest in the discovery process.** The quality of the experience we design is directly proportional to the quality of the context you share with us. Be honest about what's really going on with your team — the challenges, the tensions, the history. The more we know, the better we can design.

**Involve the right people in the brief.** The person who commissions the experience and the people who know the team best may not be the same person. Consider including a line manager, an HR business partner, or even a trusted team member in the discovery conversation.

**Trust the design.** Once the experience is designed, trust it. Avoid the temptation to add elements or change the format close to the event — bespoke designs are carefully calibrated, and late changes can undermine the coherence of the experience.

**Plan for the follow-up.** A bespoke experience, by definition, addresses something specific. Make sure you have a plan for what happens after — how the insights will be actioned, how the connections will be sustained, and how the experience will be referenced in the team's ongoing story.
