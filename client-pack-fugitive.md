### WHAT TO EXPECT ON THE DAY

You and your team will arrive for a briefing like no other. The Fugitive is a high-intensity urban pursuit experience set across Cape Town's outdoor and urban environments — and from the moment your Team Connect facilitator reveals the rules, the energy in the room will change completely.

Teams are divided into two roles: fugitives and hunters. Fugitives receive a head start and a sequence of encrypted clues leading to a network of safety points across the zone. Their mission: navigate, survive, and reach safety before time runs out. Hunters receive their own clue sequences — designed to intercept, not follow — and must coordinate their strategy to capture fugitives before they complete their route. Neither group knows exactly where the other is. That uncertainty is entirely intentional.

The experience runs across 90 to 150 minutes of active pursuit, depending on your group size and zone complexity. Throughout, your Team Connect facilitation team is positioned across the zone — managing safety, calling key moments, and ensuring the experience stays exhilarating without ever becoming dangerous.

As the time window shrinks, the pressure builds. Decision-making becomes harder. Communication becomes more critical. Leadership reveals itself. By the time everyone returns for the debrief, your team will have navigated real pressure, made real decisions, and experienced something genuinely memorable together.

The debrief brings both roles together and connects what happened in the pursuit zone to how your team leads, communicates, and performs under pressure in real working life. It is a powerful conversation.

### PRACTICAL INFORMATION

#### What to Wear

Comfortable activewear or sport casual is essential. You will be moving on foot through urban and outdoor terrain. Closed-toe, supportive shoes are mandatory — no sandals, heels, or dress shoes. Dress for the weather on the day; bring a light layer in cooler months.

#### What to Bring

* Comfortable shoes appropriate for outdoor walking and light running
* A fully charged mobile phone (required for the experience)
* Water bottle — hydration on the move is important
* Sunscreen and a hat if the experience runs in summer sunshine

#### What We Provide

* Full zone mapping and clue placement by the Team Connect facilitation team
* Safety briefing and safety team positioned throughout the zone
* Encrypted clue sequences for all teams
* Team Connect facilitator and zone coordinators throughout
* Emergency communication protocol and dedicated emergency contact
* Debrief facilitation on return

#### Dietary and Accessibility Requirements

The Fugitive is a physically active outdoor experience involving sustained walking and periods of light running across urban terrain. Please discuss any physical limitations or accessibility requirements with your Team Connect contact before booking — we will advise on whether the experience is suitable and what adaptations may be possible. Email [Info@teamconnect.co.za](mailto:Info@teamconnect.co.za).

#### Parking and Arrival

You will receive full briefing location details, parking information, and transport guidance once your zone is confirmed. Please plan to arrive at the briefing point at least 15 minutes before the scheduled start time. Do not arrive at the pursuit zone independently — all participants must be briefed together before the experience begins.

### FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: Is this experience safe?**
Safety is our highest priority in The Fugitive. The zone is meticulously mapped before every event — all road crossings, restricted areas, and risk zones are identified and managed. Your facilitation team includes dedicated safety coordinators positioned throughout the zone. A full safety briefing is conducted before the experience begins, and an emergency protocol is in place throughout. We do not run The Fugitive without thorough preparation.

**Q: Do participants need to be physically fit?**
The Fugitive involves sustained walking and periods of light running over 90–150 minutes. Participants should be comfortable with moderate physical activity. Extreme fitness is not required — the experience is won through strategy and communication, not speed alone. Please flag any health concerns with your Team Connect contact before booking.

**Q: How are the fugitive and hunter roles assigned?**
Role assignment is managed during the briefing and can be structured in multiple ways — randomly, by department, by seniority, or according to a design chosen by the client. Your Team Connect contact will discuss the best assignment approach for your specific group.

**Q: What if it rains on the day?**
The Fugitive is designed for outdoor conditions, and light rain can be managed with appropriate clothing. In the event of severe weather, your Team Connect contact will advise on rescheduling or contingency options as early as possible ahead of the event.

**Q: Is this only available in Cape Town?**
The Fugitive is primarily designed for Cape Town's urban environment, which provides ideal terrain and infrastructure for the experience. For other locations, contact your Team Connect contact to discuss feasibility and zone adaptation.

### GETTING THE MOST FROM YOUR EXPERIENCE

**Arrive ready to commit.** This is not a passive experience. The teams who get the most from The Fugitive are the ones who go in with full intent — strategic, communicative, and willing to make calls under pressure. Brief your team to treat this as a real challenge.

**Emphasise communication, not athleticism.** The fastest teams are rarely the fittest. The winning teams communicate constantly, divide responsibilities clearly, and adapt their strategy in real time. Let your team know this upfront — it changes how they approach the experience.

**Take the debrief seriously.** The pursuit is memorable; the debrief is meaningful. The conversation after the experience — where the decisions made in the zone are connected to real leadership and team dynamics — is where the day becomes genuinely useful. Make sure your team is present and engaged for it.

**Dress for the day.** Nothing limits the experience like inappropriate footwear or the wrong clothing for the weather. Communicate the dress code clearly and ensure every participant arrives prepared.
