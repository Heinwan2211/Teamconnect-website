Facilitator Guide: Escape Labs (Rouge)

Table-Top Escape Room Experience | Delivered with HintHunt | Up to 500 Participants | 3 Hours | Indoor

SECTION 1: FACILITATOR PREPARATION GUIDE

Experience Overview and Objectives

Escape Labs (Rouge) is a table-top escape room experience where teams race against the clock to crack codes, solve puzzles, and unravel a narrative mystery — all from their seats. Delivered in partnership with HintHunt, the experience is designed for large groups in indoor venues. Each table forms an independent team, and teams compete simultaneously for the fastest completion time.

Primary objectives:

  • Develop problem-solving skills under time pressure
  • Strengthen communication and delegation within small teams
  • Build competitive energy and team identity
  • Create shared experience across large, diverse groups
  • Surface natural leadership and creative thinking styles

Ideal Group Size and Duration

  • Minimum: 10 participants (2 tables of 5)
  • Maximum: 500 participants (100 tables of 5)
  • Optimal group size: 50–200 participants
  • Table size: 4–6 people per table (5 is ideal)
  • Total duration: 3 hours
    • Pre-briefing and setup: 20 minutes
    • Puzzle experience: 90 minutes
    • Break / refresh: 15 minutes
    • Awards and debrief: 35 minutes

Venue and Space Requirements

  • Indoor only — no outdoor or semi-outdoor venues
  • Minimum 1.2 m² per participant for comfortable seating
  • One table per team — standard rectangular or round banquet tables work well
  • Clear sightlines between tables (avoid pillars or partitions)
  • Large screen or projector visible to all tables for timer and leaderboard display
  • Stable Wi-Fi or dedicated hotspot (minimum 10 Mbps) for HintHunt platform
  • Adequate lighting — all tables should be well-lit for close puzzle work
  • Sound system for music, announcements, and clue audio

Recommended venue types: hotel conference centres, corporate offices with large open-plan floors, function venues, convention centres.

Equipment and Materials Checklist

HintHunt provides:

  • Puzzle kit boxes (one per table — collect from HintHunt contact prior to event)
  • Hint request cards (one set per table)
  • Leaderboard software access and credentials
  • Official scoring sheets
  • HintHunt facilitator liaison (confirm attendance 72 hours before)

Team Connect provides:

  • Timer display (connected to HintHunt leaderboard)
  • Projector or large screen(s)
  • HDMI cables and adapters
  • Wireless microphone and PA system
  • Team name cards and table markers
  • Prize envelopes (first, second, third place)
  • Pens and scratch paper (spare, per table)
  • Backup printed clue set (held by facilitator in case of tech failure)

Setup Instructions (Step by Step)

  1. Arrive at venue minimum 90 minutes before participant arrival.
  2. Confirm table configuration — one table per team, all visible to the main screen.
  3. Connect AV equipment: projector/screen, PA system, laptop.
  4. Load and test HintHunt leaderboard software — confirm it displays correctly on screen.
  5. Test Wi-Fi connectivity at every table position.
  6. Place sealed puzzle kit boxes at each table — do not open.
  7. Place hint request cards, pens, and scratch paper at each table.
  8. Place team name cards at tables.
  9. Test microphone and confirm audio reaches all corners of the room.
  10. Run a full tech check: timer display, leaderboard refresh, sound system.
  11. Brief any co-facilitators on their roles.
  12. Place prize envelopes at facilitator station — do not display until awards segment.

Facilitator Briefing Notes

  • Know the full puzzle narrative before the event — request HintHunt facilitator handover at least 24 hours prior.
  • Be familiar with all puzzle types in the current kit (cipher decoding, physical locks, logic puzzles, image challenges).
  • Understand the hint system: tables request hints; hints cost time penalties; facilitator confirms and processes all hints.
  • Manage energy with music — upbeat background tracks during puzzle time; silence or soft music during debrief.
  • Maintain a visible scoreboard at all times — competition drives engagement.
  • Watch for teams that are stuck or frustrated — proactively offer a paid hint before a team disengages.
  • Avoid revealing puzzle solutions prematurely — let teams struggle productively.

Weather/Contingency Considerations

Escape Labs is an indoor experience — weather is not a primary risk factor. However:

  • Power failure: Keep a backup offline timer and printed puzzle backup per table.
  • Tech failure (leaderboard): Manually track times on a whiteboard or flip chart.
  • Late arrivals: Assign latecomers to existing tables without disrupting the start.
  • Large no-shows: Consolidate teams to keep numbers even (maximum 6 per table).

Pre-Event Client Communication Checklist

  • Confirm final participant count (minimum 5 days before)
  • Confirm table layout preference with venue
  • Share participant information sheet with client for distribution
  • Confirm AV requirements with venue coordinator
  • Confirm HintHunt liaison attendance and arrival time
  • Confirm team name preferences or let teams choose on the day
  • Confirm awards/prizes selection with client

SECTION 2: HOSTING GUIDE

Welcome and Opening Script (Word for Word)

"Good morning / afternoon / evening, everyone — welcome to Escape Labs!

My name is [YOUR NAME] and I'll be your host today. You've just walked into one of the most competitive team experiences we run — and I want to warn you: some people walk in thinking this is just a fun puzzle game. They leave wondering how they ever underestimated their colleagues.

Here's the situation. A mystery has unfolded — details are classified until you open your kit. Your team's mission is to work through every clue, crack every code, and solve the full puzzle sequence before any other team in this room.

The team that does it fastest wins. But here's the twist — you can ask for hints. Hints cost you time on the leaderboard. Use them wisely.

You've got 90 minutes. The timer starts the moment I say go. Does anyone have questions? [Pause] Then let's find out who the sharpest minds in [COMPANY NAME] really are. Get ready... 3... 2... 1... GO!"

Rules Explanation Script

"Before we start the clock, here are your rules:

One — stay at your table. All the puzzle elements you need are in your kit.

Two — no phones for answers. You can use your phone for calculations or the event timer, but no searching clue solutions online.

Three — hints are available. If your team is stuck, hold up your hint card. A facilitator will deliver a hint. Each hint adds two minutes to your completion time.

Four — when you've solved the final puzzle, raise your hand immediately and call out your table name. A facilitator will verify your solution. If it's correct, your time is locked.

Five — the leaderboard is live on the main screen. Your table name appears the moment you complete successfully.

Any questions? No? Then let's go."

Energy Management Tips Throughout the Experience

  • Minutes 0–20: Energy is naturally high. Let it run. Play upbeat music at moderate volume.
  • Minutes 20–40: Teams settle into problem-solving. Begin roving — check in with tables that haven't progressed.
  • Minutes 40–60: Mid-point tension. Do a verbal check-in: "How are we doing, [TABLE NAME]?"
  • Minutes 60–75: Final stretch. Increase music energy. Do countdown verbal cues: "15 minutes remaining!"
  • Minutes 75–90: Sprint energy. Celebrate each completion dramatically over the microphone as teams finish.

How to Handle Reluctant Participants

  • Acknowledge that puzzles aren't for everyone — meet them with curiosity, not pressure.
  • Give reluctant participants a specific, low-stakes role: "Could you hold the clue sheet while the team talks it through?"
  • Ask them a direct question about one clue to draw them in gently.
  • If they remain disengaged, let the team's energy draw them back naturally.

How to Manage Dominant Participants

  • If one person is taking over, introduce a role structure: "Try having each person be the lead solver for one puzzle."
  • Gently redirect: "I love your energy — what does the rest of your table think?"
  • In extreme cases, speak privately: "Your team will perform even better if everyone gets a moment to contribute."

Safety Considerations

  • No physical movement required — this is a seated experience; minimal risk.
  • Ensure aisles between tables are clear for facilitator movement.
  • Confirm venue evacuation procedures with venue coordinator before the event.
  • Have first aid contact on hand per venue policy.

SECTION 3: DEBRIEF GUIDE

Why Debrief Matters for This Experience

Escape Labs is high in fun and competitive energy — without a debrief, participants leave with just the memory of winning or losing. The debrief converts the puzzle experience into insight about how the team actually operates: who leads, who listens, who freezes under pressure, who thrives.

Debrief Timing (When and How Long)

  • Deliver the debrief after the awards — let the competitive energy peak and settle before reflection.
  • Duration: 25–30 minutes
  • Optimal format: Whole group for opening questions, then pairs or table groups for deeper questions, then whole group to close.

10 Debrief Questions (From Surface to Deep)

  1. "What was the first thing your team did when you opened the kit? Who took the lead?"
  2. "At what point did your team feel most in sync? What made that happen?"
  3. "What was the hardest puzzle — and how did your team approach it differently from the first ones?"
  4. "When did you feel most frustrated? How did you manage that in the moment?"
  5. "Who on your team surprised you today — and why?"
  6. "Did your team ask for hints? What drove that decision — and who made it?"
  7. "How did your team divide the work? Was it deliberate or did it just happen?"
  8. "What would you do differently if you started again with the same team?"
  9. "Where in your actual work does your team face the same dynamics you experienced today — pressure, unclear information, competing ideas?"
  10. "What one thing from today do you want to carry back into how your team works on Monday?"

How to Connect the Experience to Workplace Behaviour

  • Puzzle-solving under time pressure mirrors project delivery under deadline.
  • Teams that succeeded usually had someone who stepped back to see the whole picture — this is a leadership metaphor worth naming.
  • Hint usage is a powerful workplace analogy: knowing when to ask for help vs. persisting alone.
  • Role differentiation that emerged naturally in the puzzle reveals existing team dynamics.

How to Close the Debrief Powerfully

"You've just spent 90 minutes under pressure, with incomplete information, in a room full of people who all wanted to win. And you figured it out.

That is not a puzzle skill. That is a team skill. The question is — how do you take that back with you?

[Pause]

I want each of you to leave today with one specific commitment. Not a vague 'we'll communicate better.' A real one. 'I will speak up earlier when I'm stuck.' 'I will let someone else lead for once.' 'I will ask for help before I'm drowning.'

Take 60 seconds and write it down. That's your Escape Labs takeaway."

What to Leave the Group With

  • A specific behavioural commitment (written during debrief)
  • The name of one teammate who surprised them today
  • The understanding that their team's best performance comes when they play to each person's natural strength

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be good at puzzles?
Not at all. Escape Labs is designed for teams, not individual experts. Different people will excel at different puzzle types — that's the point.

What if I'm competitive and my team isn't?
That energy is welcome! Your facilitator will channel it. Just remember: the best teams win together, not by one person solving everything alone.

Will I be embarrassed in front of colleagues?
Absolutely not. This is a low-pressure, high-fun experience. There's no individual performance assessment — only team performance.
